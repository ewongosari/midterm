STRING FAMILY

The largest instrument family within the orchestra is called the string family and it consists of instruments that produce sound from vibrating strings. The vibrations resonate within the wooden body of each instrument, and can be produced by plucking the strings with the fingers or being played by a bow. The violin, viola, cello and double bass are all primarily played with a bow, though sometimes the plucking technique is used. The harp is plucked and stroked with the hands. String instruments are generally placed at the front of the orchestra.

WOODWIND FAMILY

The second largest instrument family in the orchestra is named the woodwind family and is comprised of instruments that produce sound from air blown through holes in their wooden bodies. There are three groups of woodwind instruments: flutes (flutes, piccolos), single reeds (clarinets, bass-clarinets), and double reeds (oboes, English horns, bassoons, contra-bassoons). Woodwind instruments are generally situated in the middle of the orchestra, right behind the strings.

BRASS FAMILY

The third instrument family in the orchestra, the brass family, is the loudest of all other families. Each of the instruments in this family are made out of brass, hence the name, and sound is created by the vibrations of the player's lips combined with a steady stream of breath through the mouthpiece. The instruments include trumpets, trombones, French horns, and tuba. Brass instruments are usually seated in the middle of the orchestra, right behind or next to the woodwinds.

PERCUSSION FAMILY

The fourth and last instrument family within the orchestra, the percussion family, incorporates numerous amount of musical instruments that are played by striking with the hand or a stick, or by shaking or rubbing. The most commonly played instruments are timpani, bass drums, snare drums, cymbals, chimes, xylophones, triangles, and the piano. But there are many others. Unlike the other instrument families, a musician in the percussion group can play many different instruments in a single score. Percussion instruments are typically situated at the back of the orchestra.
