STRING FAMILY

Instruments: Violin, viola, cello, double bass and harp
Traits: The family with the largest number of players in the orchestra. All of its instruments produce sound from vibrating strings.
Position: At the front of the orchestra.

VIOLIN

Description: A four-stringed instrument made of many pieces of wood which are glued together. The body is hollow, thus becoming a resonating box for the sound. The strings are made of animal gut, nylon, or steel and are wrapped around pegs at one end of the instrument and attached to a tailpiece at the other. They are stretched tightly across a bridge to produce different pitches. The violin is the soprano voice in the string family.
How to play: Hold the violin under the chin, resting it on the shoulder. Use a bow to draw across the strings with one hand while pressing down on the strings with the other hand, or pluck the strings with the fingers.

VIOLA

Description: A four-stringed instrument that is slightly larger than the violin and is tuned five notes lower. It is made of many pieces of wood which are glued together. The body is hollow, thus becoming a resonating box for the sound. The strings are made of animal gut, nylon, or steel and are wrapped around pegs at one end of the instrument and attached to a tailpiece at the other. They are stretched tightly across a bridge to produce different pitches. The viola is the alto voice in the string family.
How to play: Hold the viola under the chin, resting it on the shoulder. Use a bow to draw across the strings with one hand while pressing down on the strings with the other hand, or pluck the strings with the fingers.

CELLO

Description: A four-stringed instrument that is larger than the viola and made of many pieces of wood which are glued together. The body is hollow, thus becoming a resonating box for the sound. The strings are made of animal gut, nylon, or steel and are wrapped around pegs at one end of the instrument and attached to a tailpiece at the other. They are stretched tightly across a bridge to produce different pitches. The cello is the tenor voice in the string family.
How to play: Hold the cello between the knees, resting the end rod on the floor. Use a bow to draw across the strings with one hand while pressing down on the strings with the other hand, or pluck the strings with the fingers.

DOUBLE BASS

Description: A four(or five)-stringed instrument, larger than the cello, that is made of many pieces of wood which are glued together. The body is hollow, thus becoming a resonating box for the sound. The strings are made of animal gut, nylon, or steel and are wrapped around pegs at one end of the instrument and attached to a tailpiece at the other. They are stretched tightly across a bridge to produce different pitches. The double bass has sloping shoulders, instead of rounded shoulders like the other string instruments. It is the bass voice in the string family.
How to play: Hold the double bass between the knees, resting the end rod on the floor. Use a bow to draw across the strings with one hand while pressing down on the strings with the other hand, or pluck the strings with the fingers.
Listen:

HARP

Description: Unlike the other instruments in the string family, the harp has about 45 strings stretched across its tall triangular frame. There are seven pedals at the bottom that adjust the length of the strings to produce different notes.
How to play: Rest the harp on one shoulder. Pluck or stroke the strings with the fingers of both hands while using the feet to step on the pedals.

WOODWIND FAMILY

Instruments: Flute, piccolo, clarinet, oboe, and bassoon
Traits: The family name comes from the wooden body of its intruments, although modern versions are usually made of metals or combination of wood and metals. All of its instruments produce sound from air blown through holes in the body.
Position: At the middle-front of the orchestra, right behind the strings and in front of the brass.

BRASS FAMILY

Instruments: Trumpet, trombone, French horn, and tuba
Traits: The family name comes from the metallic body of its intruments. All of its instruments produce sound from vibrations of the player's lips combined with a steady stream of breath through the mouthpiece. The brass family is the loudest in the orchestra.
Position: At the middle-back of the orchestra, right behind the woodwinds and in front of the percussion.

PERCUSSION FAMILY

Instruments: Timpani, bass drums, snare drums, cymbals, chimes, xylophones, triangles, and piano.
Traits: All of its instruments produce sound by striking with the hand or a stick, or by shaking or rubbing. Unlike the other instrument families, a musician in the percussion group can play many different instruments in a single score.
Position: At the back of the orchestra.
